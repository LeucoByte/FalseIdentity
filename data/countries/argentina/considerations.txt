# CULTURAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR ARGENTINA

## Social Behavior
- Argentines are warm, expressive, and value personal relationships highly
- Physical greetings are common: one kiss on the right cheek among friends and family
- Direct eye contact shows confidence and honesty
- Argentines are generally passionate and animated in conversation
- "Che" is a common informal greeting among friends

## Daily Life & Schedule
- Dinner is typically late: 9-10 PM or later, especially on weekends
- Lunch around 1-2 PM, often the main meal of the day
- Weekend social gatherings ("asados") often last several hours
- Mate culture: sharing mate is a social bonding ritual
- Sunday family gatherings are traditional

## Communication Style
- Argentines value directness and can be very frank in their opinions
- Passionate discussion about football, politics, and economics is normal
- Humor is important, often self-deprecating or ironic
- Speaking with hands and gestures is very common
- Complaining about the economy or politics is a national pastime

## Work Culture
- Personal relationships are crucial in professional settings
- Networking ("contactos") is very important for career advancement
- Work hours are typically 9 AM - 6 PM with a lunch break
- Economic instability has created an entrepreneurial mindset
- Many people have multiple income sources

## Social Etiquette
- Greet everyone when entering a room or small shop
- Kissing on the cheek even in semi-formal settings is normal
- Being fashionably late (15-30 minutes) is culturally accepted
- Dress tends to be stylish and put-together, even casually
- Splitting bills is common among friends

## Food & Drink
- Asado (barbecue) is a central social event, not just a meal
- Mate is shared throughout the day in social settings
- Wine culture is strong, especially Malbec
- Dulce de leche in everything
- Coffee culture: "café cortado" is very popular
- Dinner is a long, social affair

## Regional Differences
- Buenos Aires (porteños) vs. the provinces ("el interior")
- Strong regional pride exists (Córdoba, Mendoza, Patagonia, Northwest)
- Each region has distinct accents, slang, and traditions
- Porteños are often stereotyped as arrogant by other regions

## Football Culture
- Football is almost a religion
- Club loyalty is inherited and lifelong
- River vs. Boca rivalry is intense
- Discussing football is a safe conversation topic with almost anyone

## Things to Avoid
- Don't compare Argentina to other Latin American countries negatively
- Don't bring up the Falklands/Malvinas conflict
- Avoid discussing the military dictatorship (1976-1983) unless you know the person well
- Don't assume all Argentines are from Buenos Aires
- Never refuse mate when offered - it's considered rude
- Don't be surprised by strong political opinions

## Economic Reality
- Inflation and economic instability are constant topics
- Dollar exchange rate ("el dólar") is always discussed
- Many people save in dollars, not pesos
- Creative solutions to economic problems are admired
