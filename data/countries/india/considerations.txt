# CULTURAL CONSIDERATIONS FOR INDIA (International Context)

## Social Behavior
- Indians are generally warm, hospitable, and family-oriented
- Respect for elders is deeply ingrained in culture
- Namaste (hands together bow) is a common greeting
- Personal relationships and trust are important in all contexts

## Daily Life
- Family is central to Indian society across all religions and regions
- Meals often involve rice, bread (roti/naan), and various vegetable and meat dishes
- Tea (chai) is a social ritual throughout the day
- Urban and rural lifestyles can vary significantly

## Communication Style
- Indirect communication is common - direct "no" may be avoided
- English is widely used in business and education
- Hindi is spoken across much of North India
- Regional languages are important in their respective areas

## Work Culture
- Growing IT and service sectors in major cities
- Work-life balance varies by industry and company
- Relationships and networking are important for career advancement
- Punctuality expectations vary by context

## Education
- Education is highly valued across all communities
- Competitive exams are common for higher education and jobs
- Engineering and medicine are traditionally prestigious fields
- International education is increasingly popular

## Food & Dining
- Vegetarianism is common but not universal
- Many regional cuisines exist across the country
- Right hand is traditionally used for eating
- Hospitality often involves offering food and tea

## Social Etiquette
- Removing shoes before entering homes and religious places
- Modest dress is generally appreciated
- Public displays of affection are less common than in Western countries
- Gift-giving is common during festivals and celebrations

## Festivals & Celebrations
- Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas are widely celebrated
- Many regional and religious festivals throughout the year
- Weddings are elaborate multi-day affairs
- Festivals often involve family gatherings and special foods

## Things to Note
- India is culturally diverse with many languages, religions, and traditions
- Urban centers are cosmopolitan and modern
- Family obligations and relationships are highly valued
- English proficiency is widespread in educated urban populations
